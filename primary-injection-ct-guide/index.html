Variables and laws used

I is primary RMS current. B is magnetic flux density. μ₀ is permeability of free space. r is distance from conductor. N is turns. Φ is magnetic flux. f is frequency.

Biot-Savart: dB = μ₀ I dl × r̂ / 4πr²
Ampère: ∮B · dl = μ₀ I_enclosed
Faraday: V_secondary = -N dΦ/dt
Shunt: V = I × R

The best theoretical monitoring CT placement is the middle of a single straight output conductor, with the cable centered in the CT window and the jaws fully closed. Avoid lugs, bends, parallel return conductors, cabinet steel, transformer leakage fields, and excessive burden voltage near the CT knee point.
